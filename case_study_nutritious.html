Case Study - Nutritious
Nutritious is an app to tutoring adults about nutrition in an engaging way!
My Role
UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery
Project Vision
Nutritious is an app for multiple platforms in order to tutoring adults about nutrition in an entertaining way with a supportive community and to learn new recipes to be healthier.
The Problem
The main causes of death in many countries are circulatory diseases and various types of cancer. The best prevention is a healthy lifestyle but many adults don’t have good nutrition knowledge, feel lost in the information flow and are trapped in old habits
Project Goal
Create a nonjudgmental nutrition education platform for adults with a friendly community to inspire people to make healthier decisions.
My Responsibilities
Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs
User Personas
Ali
Age: 26 | Occupation: Programmer

Ali leads an active urban lifestyle and a difficult profession. He knows how important eating well is and how it affects his mood and ability to concentrate at work. He has tried several diets but has remained committed to eating meals that are made from plants, In order to continue making better decisions and maintaining his health, he is willing to try new tastes, and specially when it is done in an entertaining way.

Ellie
Age: 45 | Occupation: Employee

Ellie is a small-town resident and a mother of two children. She simply ate what she liked and was accustomed to doing until health issues began. She is currently doing internet research, taking her doctor's advice, and carefully studying how her body reacts to various foods to determine the optimum diet for her and then educate her kids about the importance of a healthy diet. Ellie engages with people on the same desire to change diet and eating habits in online forums, which she finds to be a fantastic source of comfort and encouragement.

Problem Statement

Ali is a healthy and active person who needs an entertaining content with the ability to learn more and share it with friends and family because Learning and understanding more about nutrition helps him to stay healthy and more focused.

Ellie is a mother who simply ate what she liked who needs a support and community to help her change diet because she want to change, get better and be healthier.

User Research

The focus of the user interviews was on the participants' attitudes toward healthy eating and their level of nutrition knowledge.

I was interested in finding out if any techniques were already in use, how they function, and if not, why. Finding out which digital platforms people use was crucial since I wanted to connect with a variety of age groups.

Usability Study
Study Type:
Unmoderated Usability Study
Location:
Saudi Arabia, remote
Participants:
Five participants, each completing the study individually
Length:
between 10 to 15 minutes according to the user pace.
Usability Study - Results
Users want easy access to the lessons
Users want a way to save their favorites recipes.
Users preferred clear indications of their progress.
Insights Identification and Priorities

After synthesizing the data of the usability study I found the following insights and divided the priorities from critical to noncritical.

Priority 0:
Based on the theme that: for most users, they felt overwhelmed or confused, an insight is: users need better and simpler process for what steps are required to learn
Priority 1:
Based on the theme that: for most users, they mentioned the need for a reward system to keep them motivated, an insight is: add points for each chapter, visible streak count and a leaderboard.
Priority 2:
Based on the theme that: some users mentioned they want to choose the number of serving for the ingredients an insight is: add a counter beside the ingredient to specify number of servings.

Conclusion
Impact:
Users are really interested in making better food choices, yet changing long-standing habits is very difficult. This platform offers interesting, non-judgmental nutrition learning and networking opportunities.
What I learned:
I found that, despite the size of the issue I was attempting to tackle, carefully following each phase of the design process and focusing on user requirements enabled me to develop solutions that were both workable and beneficial.
Next steps:
Add more educational resources for users to learn about nutritions.
Conduct research on how successful the app is in reaching the goal to improve users' health.
Conduct user interviews about other functionalities of the app as community and recipes to fully meet users needs.
