Case Study - Moviz
Moviz is a prototype App for everyone, including those with special requirements
My Role
UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery
Project Vision
Moviz is an app for for multiple platforms where users can reserve seats and that’s includes selections for users with special requirements or disabilities. We need to find out if the user experience, finding and reserving a seat, is easy for users to complete
The Problem
To reserve for a movie in the theater and choose the seat they needs, people with disabilities or special requirements felt excluded when they reserve a seat in movie theater apps.
Project Goal
Design a premium movie theater seat reservation app for everyone in every platform, working together with a local movie theater.
My Responsibilities
Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs
User Personas
Joseph
Age: 20 | Occupation: Student

Joseph, who has disability, is recently enrolled in college and is also taking classes at a night school for learning English. The college he attends to has the facilities for disability people and they can go anywhere in the college without any problems, but it is not always like that in other places. Joseph is passionate about movies and the outdoors and wants to explore what his neighborhood has to offer. However, when he wants to go to a movie theater, he has to reserve one of the seats in the first row because there it does not provide the facilities for disability people. Joseph’s priority is balancing his studies with fun, while also having time to not worry his family if he wants to go outside.

Ali
Age: 48 | Occupation: Employee

Ali is an employee for a business company. Although work is tough and every couple of months, he gets busy with work and goes home late. Ali loves his family and loves to spend some time with them either by staying at home or going outside to watch a movie with them. However, Ali has some difficulties, whenever he wants to go to see a movie in a theater Ali finds it difficult to reserve seats for the whole family. So, he always depends on his children to make a reservation.

Problem Statement

Joseph is a student who loves movies who needs To reserve for a movie in the theater and choose the seat he needs because Not all the seats in the theater are easily accessible for disabled people.

Ali is a father who loves his family and loves to spend some time with them who needs an easier way to reserve seats for the whole family because he finds it difficult to reserve seats through some of the apps he used before.

Competitive Analysis

The key competitors are Muvi Cinema, VOX Cinema, both are a cinema brand to the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, and SAR Saudi Railway, a construct, operate and manage a passenger and freight rail network. Muvi and VOX are a direct competitor, and SAR is an indirect competitor.

User Research

I conducted interviews with target users and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs.

Research revealed that users with accessibility needs complained about being excluded from movie theater reservation apps. Other user problems included more purchase options, food or drinks during the showtime, and QR code ticket is confusing for some users.

Usability Study
Study Type:
Unmoderated Usability Study
Location:
Saudi Arabia, remote
Participants:
Five participants, each completing the study individually
Length:
between 5 to 15 minutes according to the user pace.
Usability Study - Results

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings:

Users were looking for a “my cart” button on the screen but could not find it anywhere.
Most users felt overwhelmed or confused by the number of choices.
QR code ticket are useful for most people, but not an overwhelming majority.

Round 2 findings:

The checkout process has too many unnecessary steps
Users wants to choose between different experiecne Ex, (IMAX, Standard)
A way to see what seat that have been chosen.
Insights Identification and Priorities

After synthesizing the data of the usability study I found the following insights and divided the priorities from critical to noncritical.

Priority 0:
Based on the theme that: for most users, they felt overwhelmed or confused, an insight is: users need better and simpler process for what steps are required to reserve seat.
Priority 1:
Based on the theme that: some users got confused when, sometimes, nothing happens after clicking a button, an insight is: users need some kind of animation or notification after pressing a button.
Priority 2:
Based on the theme that: QR code ticket are useful for most people, but not an overwhelming majority, an insight is: users need more information about the potential value of QR code ticket.

Conclusion
Impact:
The app makes users feel like how the Moviz app really thinks about how to meet their needs.
One quote from peer feedback:“The app made it so easy to reserve a movie and choose any seat I want! I would definitely use this app whenever I want to go the theater alone or with friends and family ”
What I learned:
While designing the Moviz app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app's designs.
Next steps:
Talk and collaborate with other designers to learn more from them.
Conduct more user research to determine any new areas of need.
Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed.
